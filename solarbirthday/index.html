A solar (sidereal) year is 365 days, 6 hours, 9 minutes, 10 seconds — the time Earth takes to return to the same spot relative to the distant stars. That extra quarter-day nudges your solar birthday a little later each year; four of them add up to a day, so every leap year's extra day snaps it back — keeping it circling the same calendar date instead of drifting away.
